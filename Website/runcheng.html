Key Scope of Works
  • New denitrification deep-bed filter (tertiary treatment) for enhanced nitrogen removal
  • Biochemical pool converted from AO to AAO to improve TN & TP removal efficiency
  • Coarse screen well and lift pump station equipment upgraded; new reject screen installed
  • Chemical dosing room and dewatering machine room renovated — plate-frame press replaced, automatic sludge conveyor added
  • SCADA control system upgraded; transformer capacity increased to 400 kVA with backup power
  • New biological odour control system covering screen rooms and sludge handling area
  • Pump power re-evaluated and retrofitted, optimising energy efficiency and cutting electricity use by 10%

Key Problems Resolved
  • Effluent NH₃-N & TP failed new provincial standard — addressed by AAO conversion + deep-bed filter addition
  • Oversized pumps wasting energy — re-evaluated ratings and retrofitted, achieving ~10% electricity saving
  • Low aeration capacity and blocked diffusers — blowers and diffuser membranes replaced
  • Malfunctioning sludge dewatering: plate press pull-bar failure and absent auto conveyor — fully renovated
  • Single power supply risking full plant shutdown — backup power circuit and dual-supply added
  • No inlet water quality monitoring — online sensors installed for COD, NH₃-N, TP, TN, pH and flow
  • Short-circuiting in anaerobic tank reducing treatment efficiency — baffles and flow distribution retrofitted
